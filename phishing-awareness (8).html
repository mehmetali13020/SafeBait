🚩 Red Flags
1 Fake Domain Name

The URL isn't google.com. Attackers swap letters to trick you.

go0gle-secure-login.com
2 No HTTPS / Padlock

Real Google always shows a 🔒 padlock. An open lock or http:// means no encryption.

3 Arrived Via Suspicious Link

Phishing pages are reached through emails or texts — never by typing google.com yourself.

4 Identical-Looking Page

The design is copied pixel-perfect from the real site. Appearance alone means nothing.

5 Credentials Stolen

Clicking "Next" silently sent your email & password to an attacker's server.

🛡️ Always check the URL before typing  •  Navigate manually instead of clicking links  •  Enable two-factor authentication
